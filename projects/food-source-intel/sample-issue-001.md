## 🌿 What's In Season This Week

**Ramps are here.** First sightings at Union Square Greenmarket this past Saturday. Quantities are small and prices are steep (~$18-22/lb retail), but they'll be flowing by next week. If you need volume, talk to your Greenmarket vendor now — the peak window is 3-4 weeks.

**Also peaking now:**
- **Spinach** — Overwintered greens from Hudson Valley farms. Sweeter than summer spinach, incredible raw or barely wilted. $2-3/bunch at Greenmarkets.
- **Parsnips** — Frost-sweetened and dense. Last of the storage crop before spring digs. $3/lb retail, significantly less at Hunts Point.
- **Atlantic Cod** — Winter cod is at its fattiest. Fulton Fish Market vendors have had consistent supply from Georges Bank. Whole fish $4-6/lb wholesale.
- **East Coast Oysters** — Still in peak R-month form. Blue Points, Wellfleets, and Montauk Pearls all available. Wholesale prices: $0.65-1.10/ea depending on variety and volume.

## 🔍 Source Spotlight: F. Rozzo & Sons

**Category:** Premium seafood distributor
**Location:** Chelsea area, Manhattan
**Who they serve:** Michelin-starred restaurants, high-end hotels, discerning chefs
**Minimums:** Yes (contact for specifics)
**Delivery:** Daily, Manhattan and outer boroughs

**Why they matter:** Rozzo's is where many of NYC's best restaurants get their fish. They send a daily "Catch of the Day" email to accounts — an invaluable tool for menu planning around what's actually fresh, not what's always available.

**What sets them apart:**
- Cherry-picked product from Fulton and direct boat relationships
- They'll tell you when something isn't worth buying (rare for a distributor)
- Specialty items: diver scallops, live uni, exotic species
- If you're doing a tasting menu that changes daily, they're built for you

**Best for:** Fine dining, omakase, tasting menus, anyone who leads with seafood
**Not ideal for:** High-volume/low-cost operations (try Restaurant Depot or Fulton direct)

**How to get started:** Call and ask for a new account setup. Mention your restaurant and volume. They're selective but responsive to serious inquiries.

## 🆕 New Finds

**Fadaro** is worth knowing about if you run a small operation. They're an online wholesale produce distributor with **no minimum orders** — rare in NYC. Quality is solid mid-tier (not Greenmarket premium, but well above Sysco commodity). Good for filling gaps when you can't make it to Hunts Point at 3am. Website: fadaro.com

**GrowNYC's Winter Greenmarkets** are winding down their cold-storage apple and root vegetable supplies. If you want to stock up on keeper potatoes or cider apples, the next 2-3 weeks are your last window before spring crops take over.

## 📅 Seasonal Playbook: What to Do This Week

1. **Lock in ramp supply.** Talk to your Greenmarket vendor about reserving volume for the next 3 weeks. Alternatively, ask distributors like Baldor or Chef's Warehouse — they usually start carrying ramps by late March.

2. **Pre-order soft-shell crabs.** Season starts mid-April. If you want first-flush (expensive but pristine), call Element Seafood or your Fulton contact now to get on the list.

3. **Plan your spring menu transition.** Heavy winter braises → lighter preparations. The ingredient bridge: parsnips and storage roots are still excellent, but pea shoots, radishes, and asparagus arrive in 3-4 weeks.

4. **Price opportunity: winter cod.** Still at seasonal best and more affordable than summer fish. Perfect for: brandade, salt cod projects, fish & chips specials, crudo (if sashimi-grade).
