**Tweet 2 (The Problem)**
ChatGPT will tell you every idea is amazing because it's trained to be helpful.

Result: Founders waste months building things nobody wants.

90% startup failure rate isn't because execution is hard. It's because validation is broken.

**Tweet 3 (Real Validation Step 1)**
1/ Search Volume Analysis

Pull real Google data for your core problem.

Red flag: <100 monthly searches
Green flag: 1000+ with growing trend

If people aren't searching for solutions, there's no demand. Period.

**Tweet 4 (Step 2)**
2/ Competition Mapping  

Find 3-7 direct competitors.

No competition = No market (usually)
Too much competition = Oversaturated

Sweet spot: Established market with room for differentiation.

**Tweet 5 (Step 3)**  
3/ Problem Intensity Scoring

Scan Reddit, forums, reviews for pain signals:
- Frequency of complaints
- Money spent on current solutions  
- Time invested in workarounds
- Emotional language used

Scale 1-10. Anything below 6 = don't build.

**Tweet 6 (Step 4)**
4/ Customer Acquisition Reality Check

Map realistic paths to your first 100 customers.

- Where do they hang out online?
- What's the average CAC in your space?  
- Can you reach them organically?
- Do network effects exist?

**Tweet 7 (Step 5)**
5/ Monetization Viability

Research what people actually pay (not what you hope they'll pay).

Check:
- Competitor pricing
- Willingness-to-pay indicators
- LTV estimates
- Pricing model fit

**Tweet 8 (Real Example)**
Example: "Slack bot for automatic meeting notes"

✅ 8,900 monthly searches
✅ Competition exists but no Slack-native solution  
✅ 8/10 problem intensity (people hate note-taking)
✅ Clear acquisition path (Slack directory)
✅ $10-25/month proven pricing

Verdict: Build it.

**Tweet 9 (The Results)**
Founder launched 3 weeks later.
$2K MRR by month 2.

This is what data-driven validation looks like.

Not "believe in yourself."
Not "just ship it."
Real market intelligence.
