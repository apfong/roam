# Product Naming: Validation Reports Service

## Service Overview
- Founders submit startup/product ideas
- AI agents run market validation (Reddit signals, competitor analysis, demand data, willingness-to-pay evidence)
- Professional validation report delivered in 24 hours
- Pricing: $49 basic, $99 comprehensive
- Under Oddly Useful umbrella brand

## Requirements
- Short, memorable, punchy
- .com or .io available
- Evokes: clarity, truth, signal vs noise, validation, proof, confidence
- NOT generic AI branding
- Works as product name and potential standalone brand
- Reference style: Stripe, Notion, Linear, Vercel

## Evaluation Criteria
- **Verb Usage**: Can people naturally say "I should [name] this idea"?
- **Tweetability**: Easy to recommend and share?
- **Brand Fit**: Pairs well with Oddly Useful parent brand?
- **Memorability**: Sticky and distinctive?
- **Concept Fit**: Evokes validation/clarity/truth?

## Top 5 Finalists

### 1. Pulse
**Reasoning**: Perfect for "market pulse" - evokes real-time signals, heartbeat of market demand. Natural verb usage ("pulse this idea"). Single syllable, memorable, sophisticated.
**Verb test**: "I should pulse this idea first" ✓
**Tweetability**: "Just pulsed my startup idea - the market signals are strong!" ✓

### 2. Signal  
**Reasoning**: Directly addresses core value prop - cutting through noise to find real market signals. Strong tech brand feel (like Linear, Vercel). Clear action verb.
**Verb test**: "Let me signal this concept" ✓  
**Tweetability**: "Signal helped me validate my idea in 24 hours" ✓

### 3. Scope
**Reasoning**: Professional, implies comprehensive market scoping. Natural business verb ("scope the market"). Suggests thoroughness and precision.
**Verb test**: "I need to scope this market first" ✓
**Tweetability**: "Scope gave me the validation data I needed" ✓

### 4. Proof
**Reasoning**: Direct validation concept - provides "proof" of market demand. Strong, confident, results-oriented. Simple but powerful.
**Verb test**: "I should proof this concept" ✓ (slightly less natural)
**Tweetability**: "Got my market proof in 24 hours" ✓

### 5. Gauge
**Reasoning**: Implies measurement and assessment of market potential. Professional feel, natural business usage. Suggests precision and data-driven approach.
**Verb test**: "Let me gauge this market" ✓
**Tweetability**: "Gauge helped me understand my market" ✓

## Domain Availability Research

**❌ Signal**: signal.org taken by Signal messaging app (major brand conflict)
**❌ Proof**: proof.io taken by ESG impact platform, useproof.com exists  
**❌ Gauge**: gauge.io taken by UX consultancy, gauge.org by test automation framework
**⚠️ Pulse**: Multiple pulse-related domains exist, may be premium/expensive
**🔍 Scope**: Needs further verification

## Revised Top 5 Recommendations

Given domain availability constraints, here are the strongest candidates:

### 1. **Scope** 
**Why**: Professional, implies comprehensive market scoping. Natural verb usage. Less crowded domain space.
**Verb test**: "I need to scope this market first" ✓
**Alternative domains**: scope.market, scope.tools if .com/.io unavailable

### 2. **Echo** 
**Why**: Market feedback, response signals. Evocative of listening to market voice.
**Verb test**: "Let me echo this idea" ✓  
**Likely availability**: Less common in business tools space

### 3. **Crisp**
**Why**: Clear, precise, definitive results. Modern tech feel.
**Verb test**: "I should crisp this concept" ✓ (somewhat natural)
**Brand fit**: Clean, sharp, decisive

### 4. **Sharp**
**Why**: Precision, clarity, focused insights. Strong business connotations.
**Verb test**: "Let me sharp this idea" (less natural but workable)
**Alternative phrasing**: "Get sharp insights on..."

### 5. **Prime** 
**Why**: First/best validation, premium quality. Suggests thoroughness.
**Verb test**: "Prime this idea first" ✓
**Brand positioning**: Premium validation service
