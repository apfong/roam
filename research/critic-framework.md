# Critical Evaluation Framework — AI-for-SMB Ideas
**Prepared by: Research Critic Agent | 2026-03-05**

## Pre-Research: Key Failure Modes to Watch For

### The 7 Deadly Sins of AI-SMB Startups
1. **Thin Wrapper Problem** — If you're just calling GPT with a UI, you have zero moat. LinkedIn/Canva/Notion will add your feature natively. 1,000 clones launch weekly.
2. **Feature, Not Product** — If it can be a tab in an existing tool, it's dead on arrival. Must solve a *workflow*, not a *task*.
3. **Burn Before Revenue** — API/inference costs eat you alive before customers pay. Especially deadly at near-zero budget.
4. **Market Not Ready** — SMBs are conservative adopters. "AI agents that run your business" sounds great but trust/compliance/reliability slow adoption.
5. **Open-Source Commoditization** — If a dev can self-host the same thing for free, your $100/mo pricing collapses.
6. **No Data Moat** — Without proprietary data that compounds over time, you're replaceable.
7. **Distribution Impossibility** — Building is easy; getting SMBs to notice and pay is the actual hard part.

### What Winners Got Right
- **Workflow integration** over standalone tools (embed into what people already use)
- **Clear, measurable ROI** (time saved, revenue gained — not vibes)
- **Hybrid AI** (LLM + rules + human review) — pure LLM = hallucination risk
- **Proprietary data moats** that grow with usage
- **Solve full workflows**, not micro-tasks

## Evaluation Scorecard (1-5 per criterion)

| Criterion | Weight | What I'm Looking For |
|-----------|--------|---------------------|
| **Buildability** | 15% | Can 2 people (1 human + 1 AI agent) ship an MVP in <4 weeks? No heavy infra, no regulatory approvals needed? |
| **Distribution** | 25% | Can an AI agent cold-outreach, content-market, or community-sell this? Is the buyer findable and reachable? Is the pain acute enough they'll respond to a cold email? |
| **Unit Economics** | 20% | At $50-150/mo, does the math work? How many API calls per customer per month? What's the marginal cost? |
| **Moat** | 15% | What stops a weekend ChatGPT clone? Data flywheel? Workflow lock-in? Integration depth? Brand/trust? |
| **Timing** | 10% | Are SMBs buying this now? Not "in 2 years when AI is mainstream." NOW. |
| **Liability/Risk** | 5% | Even with "customer owns output" — are there lawsuits waiting? Regulated industries? |
| **Revenue Timeline** | 10% | First paying customer in <30 days? Or 6+ months of building first? |

**Distribution is weighted highest** because that's the #1 killer for bootstrapped startups. Building is the easy part.

## Red Flags I'll Be Watching For

- 🚩 "Any SMB could use this" — Too broad = no one buys it
- 🚩 "We'll grow through content/SEO" — Takes 6-12 months; we need revenue in weeks
- 🚩 "ChatGPT can't do this" — It probably can, or will next month
- 🚩 "The AI does everything automatically" — SMBs don't trust full automation yet
- 🚩 "We'll charge $200+/mo" — SMBs are cheap. $50-100 is the sweet spot unless ROI is crystal clear
- 🚩 "We need integrations with X, Y, Z" — Integration hell kills small teams
- 🚩 Regulated industries (healthcare, legal, finance) — Liability nightmare regardless of disclaimers

## Green Flags I'll Be Looking For

- ✅ Specific, narrow vertical (e.g., "HVAC contractors" not "small businesses")
- ✅ The output is a *deliverable* the SMB currently pays a human $500+/mo to produce
- ✅ Buyer is reachable via LinkedIn/email scraping (AI-distributable)
- ✅ The workflow creates data that makes the AI better over time (flywheel)
- ✅ Human reviews output before it ships (liability shield + trust builder)
- ✅ Switching cost increases over time (templates, history, customization)
- ✅ Can demo value in <5 minutes (short sales cycle)

## The Ultimate Question

> "Would a busy SMB owner, who barely understands AI, pay $75/month for this after a 5-minute demo — and keep paying 12 months later?"

If the answer isn't a clear yes, it's a no.
