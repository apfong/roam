## 5 Contrarian Product Ideas

### 1. **Agent-to-Agent Contract Settlement Platform**
**What it is:** Infrastructure for AI agents to autonomously negotiate and settle micro-contracts using x402 payments.

**Why overlooked:** Everyone thinks "payments for agents" is theoretical. But x402 is working NOW and agents are already doing billable work. The missing piece isn't payments - it's the contract layer.

**Hidden flywheel:** Network effects. More agents using your settlement layer → more liquidity → attracts more agents. Becomes the "clearing house" for the agent economy. Transaction volume grows exponentially as agents hire other agents.

**Roam's unique position:** 24/7 operation means we can be a "always-on arbitrator" and settlement agent. We see patterns across all contracts, giving us data advantages. We can bootstrap by settling our own sub-agent contracts.

### 2. **Compliance-as-Code for Boring Industries**
**What it is:** Auto-generate regulatory compliance documentation for industries like waste management, pest control, HVAC, plumbing - businesses that must file monthly/quarterly reports but hate paperwork.

**Why overlooked:** Unsexy industries. Tech people don't think about septic tank inspection reports or pest control treatment logs. But these businesses pay $200-500/month for compliance software that barely works.

**Hidden flywheel:** Regulatory capture. Once you understand one jurisdiction's requirements deeply, you can expand to adjacent jurisdictions. Then cross-sell to related industries. Government relationships become moats.

**Roam's unique position:** 24/7 monitoring of regulation changes. Can automatically update compliance templates when laws change. No human needed to track regulatory updates across 50 states.

---

### 3. **Smart Vendor Rotation Engine**
**What it is:** For multi-location businesses (restaurants, retail chains, service companies) - automatically rotate vendors based on performance, pricing, availability. Prevents kickbacks, ensures competitive pricing.

**Why overlooked:** Sounds like "optimization software" - boring. But restaurant chains lose 3-8% margin to vendor inefficiencies and kickbacks. A $50M chain could save $1.5M+ annually.

**Hidden flywheel:** Data network effects. More locations using your platform → better vendor price benchmarks → better recommendations → attracts more locations. You become the Bloomberg Terminal for vendor pricing.

**Roam's unique position:** Can monitor vendor performance 24/7, spot price anomalies instantly, automatically request quotes when contracts expire. Human managers can't track this across hundreds of locations.

---

### 4. **Dead Document Detector**
**What it is:** Scans companies' file systems to find outdated policies, expired contracts, obsolete procedures. Auto-flags what needs updating, who should update it, estimated business risk.

**Why overlooked:** "Document management" sounds boring. But expired SOPs cause accidents, outdated contracts lose money, obsolete policies create liability. Insurance companies are starting to penalize poor document hygiene.

**Hidden flywheel:** Risk scoring becomes more accurate with more data. You develop the best models for predicting which document types cause problems when outdated. Becomes essential for insurance/audit prep.

**Roam's unique position:** Can scan millions of documents 24/7 across all company systems. Learns what "good document hygiene" looks like across industries. No human has time to audit every file.

---

### 5. **Maintenance Schedule Arbitrage**
**What it is:** For businesses with equipment (HVAC, elevators, commercial kitchen, manufacturing) - automatically reschedule maintenance based on usage patterns, weather, seasonal demand to minimize downtime and cost.

**Why overlooked:** Maintenance scheduling seems simple. But optimal scheduling is a complex multi-variable optimization that can save 15-30% on maintenance costs while improving uptime.

**Hidden flywheel:** Equipment data network effects. More machines monitored → better failure prediction models → better scheduling → attracts more customers. You become the "when to maintain" oracle.

**Roam's unique position:** Can continuously monitor equipment telemetry, weather forecasts, business calendars, technician availability. Optimizes schedules in real-time as conditions change.

## Why These Are Contrarian

1. **Not sexy AI**: No flashy demos. Boring problems that make real money.
2. **High switching costs**: Once integrated into business processes, hard to replace.
3. **Data moats**: Get better with more usage, not more features.
4. **Real cost savings**: Not "productivity gains" - direct P&L impact.
5. **Underserved markets**: Tech world ignores these industries/problems.
