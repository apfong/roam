# CLM-Lite Pricing Analysis

*Research date: March 6, 2026*

## Current Market Pricing Landscape

### What freelancers/SMBs currently pay for contract-adjacent tools:

| Tool | Price Range | What You Get |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Bonsai** | $0–$79/mo | Contracts + invoicing + proposals (basic) |
| **HoneyBook** | $36–$129/mo | CRM + contracts + payments (basic contracts) |
| **Dubsado** | $20–$40/mo | Forms + contracts + invoicing |
| **PandaDoc** | $0–$49/user/mo | Document creation + e-signatures |
| **DocuSign** | $10–$40/user/mo | E-signatures only |
| **Notion** | $0–$10/user/mo | DIY database (no contract features) |

### What SMBs pay for actual CLM:

| Tool | Price Range | Gap from freelancer tools |
|------|-----------|--------------------------|
| **ContractSafe** | $375+/mo ($4,500/yr) | 10-19x more expensive |
| **Concord** | $399+/mo ($4,800/yr) | 10-20x more expensive |
| **Contractbook** | $400+/mo ($4,800/yr) | 10-20x more expensive |
| **ContractWorks** | $600+/mo ($7,200/yr) | 15-30x more expensive |

### Enterprise CLM (for reference):
- Ironclad: $25K–$150K/yr
- Juro: ~$34,500/yr avg
- Icertis: $100K+/yr

## Price Sensitivity Evidence

1. **Reddit users actively seek "low cost" solutions** — multiple threads asking for affordable contract management, getting answers like "just use a spreadsheet"
2. **HoneyBook 89% price increase backlash** — users vocally upset about $19→$36 starter plan increase
3. **Notion/Sheets as workarounds** — users spending hours building DIY systems rather than paying $375+/mo for real CLM
4. **ContractSafe at $375/mo described as "affordable"** in the CLM space — shows how distorted enterprise pricing is
5. **Bonsai at $24/mo is "priced less than competition"** per reviews — the floor for freelancer tools

## Willingness to Pay Signals

- Freelancers already pay $24-$40/mo for Bonsai/HoneyBook (basic contracts)
- Small businesses already pay $45-$57/user/mo for Zoho One
- The Notion + PandaDoc combo people are building costs $10-$59/mo
- People explicitly asking for "low cost CRM/Contract Management" — implies budget exists, just not at $375+

## Recommended Pricing for CLM-Lite

### Proposed Tiers:

| Plan | Price | Target | Features |
|------|-------|--------|----------|
| **Free** | $0/mo | Hook | 5 contracts, basic repository, 1 user |
| **Solo** | $12/mo | Freelancers | 25 contracts, templates, renewal reminders, e-signatures |
| **Team** | $29/mo | Small teams (up to 5) | Unlimited contracts, version tracking, AI clause review, collaboration |
| **Business** | $59/mo | Growing SMBs (up to 15) | Everything + API, advanced analytics, custom workflows |

### Pricing Rationale:
1. **$12/mo Solo** — 50% less than Bonsai's starter, but focused on contract lifecycle (not invoicing/proposals)
2. **$29/mo Team** — Below the psychological $30 threshold, 13x cheaper than cheapest real CLM (ContractSafe)
3. **$59/mo Business** — Still cheaper than HoneyBook Premium, but with actual CLM features
4. **Free tier** — Critical for adoption; PandaDoc's free tier drives massive top-of-funnel

### Revenue Model:
- At $29 ARPU with 1,000 customers = $29K MRR = $348K ARR
- At $29 ARPU with 5,000 customers = $145K MRR = $1.74M ARR
- Potential add-ons: AI contract review ($5-10/mo), premium templates, API access
